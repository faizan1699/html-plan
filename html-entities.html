HTML Entities & Special Characters

Home | Entities

What are HTML Entities?

HTML entities are special codes used to display characters that have special meaning in HTML or are hard to type on a keyboard.

Common Reserved Characters

These characters have special meaning in HTML, so you need entities:

Character Entity Name Entity Number Example
< &lt; &#60; <
> &gt; &#62; >
& &amp; &#38; &
" &quot; &#34; "
' &apos; &#39; ' 
<p>Use &lt; for less than < and &gt; for greater than ></p>
<p>The ampersand is written as &amp;</p>
<p>Quotes: &quot;Hello&quot; and &apos;Hi&apos;</p>

Spacing Entities

Non-breaking space (keeps words together):

Hello World (these words won't break apart)

<p>Hello&nbsp;World</p>

En space (half the width of em space):

Hello World

<p>Hello&#8196;World</p>

Em space (wider space):

Hello World

<p>Hello&#8195;World</p>

Copyright & Symbols

  • Copyright: © &copy; or &#169;
  • Registered: ® &reg; or &#174;
  • Trademark: ™ &trade; or &#8482;
<p>&copy; 2025 My Website</p>
<p>Company Name&reg;</p>
<p>Product Name&trade;</p>

Currency Symbols

  • Euro: € &euro; or &#8364;
  • Pound: £ &pound; or &#163;
  • Yen: ¥ &yen; or &#165;
  • Cent: ¢ &cent; or &#162;
  • Dollar: $ (can type directly, or &#36;)
<p>Price: &euro;50</p>
<p>Cost: &pound;30</p>
<p>Amount: &yen;1000</p>

Math Symbols

  • Plus/Minus: ± &plusmn;
  • Times: × &times;
  • Division: ÷ &divide;
  • Not equal: ≠ &ne;
  • Less than or equal: ≤ &le;
  • Greater than or equal: ≥ &ge;
  • Infinity: ∞ &infin;
<p>5 &plusmn; 2 = 3 or 7</p>
<p>4 &times; 3 = 12</p>
<p>10 &divide; 2 = 5</p>
<p>x &ne; y</p>

Arrows

  • Left: ← &larr;
  • Right: → &rarr;
  • Up: ↑ &uarr;
  • Down: ↓ &darr;
  • Left-Right: ↔ &harr;
<p>Go &larr; left or &rarr; right</p>
<p>Scroll &uarr; up or &darr; down</p>

Greek Letters

  • Alpha: α &alpha;
  • Beta: β &beta;
  • Gamma: γ &gamma;
  • Pi: π &pi;
  • Omega: Ω &Omega;
<p>The Greek letter alpha is &alpha;</p>
<p>Pi equals &pi;</p>
